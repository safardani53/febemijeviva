Fore Street car park in Bodmin has a confusing sign baffling motorists

June 09, 2024
blog

THIS could be Britain's most confusing car park sign - as motorists have no idea if they should pay or can park for free.

Cornish drivers find themselves stumped when they approach the sign, noticed by Emily Nicholls, which offers "free parking" but also asks people to get a ticket from a machine.

She said of the sign at the Fore Street car park in Bodmin: "It's utterly confusing - its unclear whether free parking is actually available or not, and how long you can leave your car there for.

"And it says you cannot get a ticket without putting money in the machine anyway."

She sent a photo of the confusing sign to Cornwall Council.

More road sign confusion: Earlier this year, motorist Eddie Mustoe won his case against Bristol City Council which took almost a year, insisting a no entry sign which caught him out is ambiguous.

This comes as free parking is being offered in some car parks as a project begins to turn Bodmin into the first official cycle town.

The council has warned there will be months of disruption because of roadworks linked to the bike scheme, and many locals are planning to protest.

Stewart Quinney from St Mabyn works for a scaffolding company and is one of the protest organisers.

"Businesses are worried about trade.

"For the projects we've got coming up over the eleven month period, if I can't get into Bodmin or through Bodmin and I've got to take the twenty-six mile tailback.

"Personally it's going to cost me around thirty thousand pounds. I think it's similar for the other companies in Bodmin that supply the building materials.

"Nobody knew the extent of the road closures. This scheme should've been a three year project and they're pushing it through, just shutting every road you can access.

"Even the taxi drivers - we had two of the main companies attend the meeting - are saying that it will not work."

Tim Moor, Project Manager for the Bodmin Cycle Scheme, said "We understand there will be some inconveniences to say the least to businesses and properties in and around Church Square and Dennison Road in particular, and we are doing every we can to try and mitigate this problem.

"We are introducing free car parking for nearby car parks. We are trying to promote Bodmin as open as usual."
