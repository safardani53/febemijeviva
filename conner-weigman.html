The player grew up in his homeland and attended Bridgeland High School, where he imprinted his potential as a multi-athlete (a baseballer & a footballer).

Conner Weigman | High School Career, Stats & Accolades

The Texas-born attended Bridgeland High School, where he played under coach David Raffield. A four-star prospect as the No.1 quarterback by Rivals and ESPN, Conner Weigman got his start as Q.B. during his sophomore season.

As a sophomore, he became Texas District 14-6A Offensive Newcomer of the Year in 2019.

His junior season concluded with 3,808 passing yards and 42 touchdowns against 11 interceptions. It earned him the honor of the 2020 District 16-6A Offensive Player of the Year.

As a senior, Weigman became the Texas District 16-6A Offensive MVP (2021)- with 34 touchdowns and nearly 2,900 total yards. Moreover, the player took Bridgeland to the 6A Region II championship match.

He was voted the Houston Touchdown Club Offensive Player of the Year for leading the high school team Bears to the state quarterfinals. It followed him into the 2022 Under Armour All-American and the Elite-11 Finals.

In December 2021, Conner Weigman won the National High School Quarterback of the Year. He was the second Houston-area player to win praise after Vince Young (in 2001).

The player chose Texas A&M over offers from Arkansas, Oklahoma, Baylor, Florida, and Texas to play college football.

College Football Over Baseball

While at high school, Weigman demonstrated himself as a multi-athlete prospect with potential for baseball and football.

As observers were eyeing him as probable a ‘first- or second-round baseball guy,’ he left out the MLB option for good.

During the final five months at Bridgeland High School, he was weighing on offers for college football- hence, de-facto bypassing an offer to play Bridgeland baseball team.

He later signed with Texas A&M to play for the Aggies and withdrew his name from the 2022 Major League Baseball draft.

Conner Weigman | College Career At Texas A&M Aggies 

Weigman received scholarships from colleges like Baylor, Michigan, Virginia, Mississippi, Oklahoma, Missouri, Arkansas, and Florida. It is no surprise, given that he is a five-star prospect of the 2022 class, as per 247 Sports.

But opting out of those, he committed to Texas A&M University on February 04, 2021.

On December 15 of that year, he signed a letter of intent and enrolled later on January 11, 2022, as a freshman for the 2022 season with the Aggies.

He would join the 2022 recruits Evan Stewart, Gabe Brownlow-Dindy, Kam Dewberry, Denver Harris, and Andrew Maleski.

Conner ranked #2 as the top quarterback recruit behind Cade Klubnik and ahead of the Top 5, Ty Simpson, Drew Allar, and Walker Howard.

Moreover, he ranked #22 overall in of Top 100 recruits of 2022, while the first five being Travis Hunter, Walter Nolen, Luther Burden, Mykel Williams, and Domani Jackson.

Debut As Aggies Freshman

The player made his collegiate debut versus South Carolina as a replacement for injured Haynes King at South Carolina.

This performance may have significantly impacted head coach Jimbo Fisher’s choice. The player completed 8/15 passes for 91 yards.

His first start was against Ole Miss (28-34 loss) on October 29, 2022, compiling 338 yards on 28-of-44 passing while connecting on four touchdowns to create a new A&M true freshman record.

The Texas A&M Aggies feature notable young figures like McKinnley Jackson (DL), Ainias Smith (WR), Amari Daniels (RB), and Blake Smith (TE), among others.
