What happened to Chuck on Street Outlaws?

April 18, 2024
blog

CHUCK Seitsinger has been on the popular Discovery show since season one.

But fans were shocked to learn that the Street Outlaws star had spent time behind bars earlier this year.

Who is Chuck on Street Outlaws?

Chuck is one of the racing veterans on the reality TV show Street Outlaws.

He was born on February 18, 1970, in Edmond, Oklahoma.

He now resides in Mustang, Oklahoma.

From a young age, Chuck would watch street racing shows in his local city.

He has spent the majority of his fifty years racing, and he began driving as soon as he was old enough during his senior year.

In 2004, Chuck was crowned Rookie of the Year by the NOPI Drag Racing Association and won the Nitrous Express P/4 XBOX Cup Championship in the 4 Cylinder division.

Chuck runs a successful repair shop, Advanced Motorsports and has two daughters from his previous marriage.

What happened to Chuck on Street Outlaws?

In a YouTube video posted to his channel titled "Chuck's out of Jail Judges and Grudges" on May 2, 2022, Chuck revealed that he had been arrested.

He was arrested on a misdemeanour charge of "two counts including making a threat of violence."

Both offences reportedly took place on July 25, 2020.

"Long story short, [the children] needed help and I was in the hospital with Covid. The kids were with me all summer. I got Covid really bad and got sent to the hospital in ICU.

"Their mother made them go home. They had problems over there. Things happened and I got a call. Being in the ICU, I wasn't going to wipe anyone's a-- or anything," Chuck said.

He continued: "Here we are a year and a half later, we were fixing this car, and I got a misdemeanour charge filed on me. I was supposed to go into court that morning and sign papers for a no contest for the misdemeanour plea.

"We had agreed with the DA, but for whatever reason, the judge thought I needed a harder sentence and gave me a year of probation. Then, he sentenced me to 60 days in jail."

Did Chuck go to jail?

In the YouTube video, Chuck explained that while he was sentenced to 60 days in jail, he served only 25.

He was also required to complete a 13-week anger management course and pay court costs.

Chuck's case officially closed on April 21, 2022.
