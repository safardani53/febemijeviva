Love Island's Ron Hall reveals surprise career move as he opens up about 'imposter syndrome'

blog

LOVE Island star Ron Hall has opened up about his surprise career move as he plans to become the next Martin Lewis.

The financial advisor, 25, may be following in the footsteps of the Good Morning Britain star, 50, as he plans to use his platform to help younger people with money advice.

Ron - who found love with celebrity make-up artist Lana Jenkins - said he wants to make a difference and "do his bit" amid the cost-of-living crisis.

Speaking exclusively to The Sun, Ron said: "For me I could not care less about this following stuff, I just don't think it means anything.

"I loved what I did before, I went into the villa - I had everything sorted for me except a girlfriend - finding someone was the thing to complete it.

"I never went to uni - I sat in an office for seven years and had done nothing with my life, so I went on Love Island for an experience.

"Now I've come out and I'm almost treating it as a gap year and using this platform.

"A lot of people on Instagram want me to talk about money, everyone knows the state of the economy at the minute, people are struggling."

When asked if he could be the next Martin Lewis, Ron laughed: "I'm toying with the idea, I don't think young people have someone of the similar age helping them.

"A lot of people think finance is hard but someone my age who can help people who are 18 to 35 and just help them plan for their future, and the importance of money.

"I think it would be a great route to gown down, talking about money and being able to help people."

Ron - who lost the sight in his right eye in a footballing accident as a youngster - said he also wants to help those with disabilities after being inundated with messages from people in a similar situation.

"I also think there needs to be more conversation about disability and mental health and talk about people losing relatives," he continued.

"I get so many messages from people asking me about their relatives - so many inspirational one saying 'my daughter or son watched you'. I guess it's an insecurity that you look different and I try and tell them to embrace it.

"I've had so many people come up to me and said they had a prosthetic eye and said they are scared to drive.

"I'd really like to do a podcast or a YouTube - I've sent video messages talking about how I do it, how I experience it."

The Essex lad said he'd have no hesitation to return to his successful job later down the line.

"If I'm going to do anything with this new platform I'm going to do my bit and however far that takes me and if I stop enjoying it then I'm going back to my successful job that I had," he added.

Ron - who won over the hearts of the nation during his time on the ITV2 dating show - said he's experienced "imposter syndrome" since leaving the villa but is learning to embrace this new life.

Despite coming as runners-up on one of Britain's biggest reality shows, Ron admitted that he doesn't see himself as famous.

He said: "I'll go to these events and be like 'why am I here?', the weirdest thing in the world is when people come up to me and be like 'can I take a picture', that blows my mind. I'm actually like really, why?

"I'm not a big star - Drake's a big star, Liam Payne's a big star. I'm not a big star.
