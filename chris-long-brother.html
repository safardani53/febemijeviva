Chris Long Brother | Early Life & Education

The NFL guard Kyle Long was born on December 5, 1988, in Ivy, Virginia, United States, to the Super Bowl XVIII champion Howie Long and Diane Addonizio on March 28, 1985.

The middle child of his parents, Kyle’s childhood was spent in affluence, given the fact that his father was a star player of his time.

HE grew up along with his two brothers, Chris Long and Howie Long Jr. both of whom are associated WITH football.

Chris played in the NFL for 11 seasons and is two times Super Bowl champion, and Howie Long Jr. is a scouting assistant and personnel assistant for the Raiders.

Kyle inherited his love for football and required skills from his father. And since his older brother followed in his father’s footsteps, it only fueled Kyle’s desire to prove himself on the field.

Even though his true love was football, he played a bunch of different sports in high school.

Education

He went to St. Anne’s-Belfield School in Charlottesville, Virginia, just like his older brother. As a high schooler, Kyle played both football and baseball.

Although he wanted to pursue football professionally, he also excelled in baseball, which took him to the point where the Chicago White Sox drafted him in the 23rd round of the 2008 Major League Baseball Draft.

However, a man of his word, Kyle honored his commitment to Florida State University and did not sign with the team.

Kyle left FSU following DWI charges in 2009 and enrolled at Saddleback College in Mission Viejo, California, in 2010.

The following year Kyle transferred to the University of Oregon and played for the Oregon Ducks football team.

Chris Long Brother | Career

Kyle Long was drafted by the Chicago Bears 20th overall in the 2013 NFL Draft.

With this selection, he became the first guard to be drafted by the team in the first round since Roger Davis in 1960.

Kyle started the 2013 season opener against the Bengals at right guard. This made him the first rookie in the team’s history to start a season opener in the Super Bowl era at that position.

He also received three consecutive Pro Bowl selections from 2013 to 2015.

On September 7, 2016, Kyle and Bears agreed on a four-year contract extension.

After that, he practically spent all his time off the field because of continuous injuries. On January 6, 2020, Kyle announced his retirement from football.

Nonetheless, the Bears declined his contract option for 2020, which made Kyle an unrestricted free agent in March of the same year.

Exactly after a year, Kansas City Chiefs signed Kyle after knowing his intention to come out of retirement.

Still, luck was not in his favor, as injuries never left Kyle.

Chiefs last activated Long on December 27, 2021. He is currently a free agent.

About Chris Long

Chris Long had an astonishing college football. He played for the Virginia Cavaliers for four years, amassing numerous awards and setting several records.

He received a first-place vote and finished 10th in the Heisman Trophy voting in 2007.

Chris forwent his senior year at college and declared for a draft.

Although he could not lift bench-press due to an injured thumb during his NFL Combine, St. Louis Rams drafted Chris second overall in the 2008 NFL Draft.

Long recorded his first career sack against the Giants quarterback Eli Manning on September 14, 2008.

At the end of the 2014 season, Long sustained an ankle injury limiting his season to just six games.

In the 2015 season, he played only 12 games and was successively released by the team on February 19, 2016.

The next month Long agreed to a one-year deal with the Patriots.

He went on to win Super Bowl LI with the Patriots.

After a season with the Patriots, Chris signed a two-year deal with the Philadelphia Eagles on March 28, 2017, on his 32nd birthday.

With the Eagles, Long won his second Super Bowl, Super Bowl LII. After the 2018 season, he announced his intention to retire, and on May 18, 2019, Long hung his boots.

A Little About Howie Long

The Pro Football Hall of Famer and one-time Super Bowl champion Howie Long played in the NFL for 13 seasons.

Howie spend his whole 13 seasons with the Raiders franchise in Oakland and Los Angeles.

After more than a decade-long football career amassing tons of awards, Long hung his boots after the 1993 season and pursued broadcasting and acting.

As of now, he serves as a studio analyst for Fox Sports’ NFL coverage. Howie was drafted by the Oakland Raiders in the second round of the 1981 NFL Draft.

After finishing his NFL career, Long pursued an acting career and focused mostly on action films. Howie has appeared in films including Firestorm, Broker Arrow, and 3000 Miles to Graceland.

As for his personal life, Howie married Diane Addonizio in 1982. The pair met during his freshman year at Villanova.

They had three sons together; Chris, Kyle, and Howie Jr.
