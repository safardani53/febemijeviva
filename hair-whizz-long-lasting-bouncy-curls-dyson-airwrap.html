ANYONE with a Dyson Airwrap will know that it can take a a fair bit of practice to achieve the desired hair results you're after.

But if you're yet to nail the perfect technique, then no fear...because hair whizz Lillian Babaian, who has "naturally super straight hair," has shared her top tips and tricks that "gets you instant volume and curls for days."

In a clip shared to TikTok, she begins by explaining that the first thing to do is to apply mousse to damp hair, which will "allow your curls to last for days."

"You want to first go in with the pre-dryer attachment and make sure that your hair is 80 to 90% dry," the hair guru says.

"When using this tool, lift your roots up to add maximum volume."

Lillian goes on to say that the next step depends on your hair type.

"If you're anything like me and your hair tends to hold curl better when it's a little bit more on the dryer end, I'll lightly go in with a smoothing attachment and just quickly go through each strand and dry it a little bit more before going in with the barrels," she explains.

However, Lillian points out that if you think your hair needs to be a little bit more on the damper side, then skip this and go straight into the next step.

With the barrels attached, she then reveals the key part.

"You want to curl each piece away from the face always for that va va voom 90s look," she explains.

Starting on the left side, Lillian continues: "You're going to wrap each piece of hair around the barrel.

"Once the hair is wrapped and suctioned around the barrel, towards the end, you're going to use the cool shot button.

"The cool shot button is what's going to give your curl that lasting effect.

"And as long as your curls cool, that's what sets it in place."

The hair whizz advises not to brush anything out yet - adding that you need to do both sides and leave it until you get to the top section of the hair.

"We're going to let these curls just sit here and cool," she explains.

"I'm going to switch barrels and do the rest of the hair until I get to the crown.

"At this point, we're going to opt in for rollers."

She continues: "Now that we've curled the rest of the hair, the front pieces I sectioned out, we're going to go in with two rollers and use the brush attachment, rolling and brushing it upwards.

"Then we're going to clip it up for 10 to 15 minutes to cool."

After removing the rollers, she then brushes out the rest of her hair, revealing the stunning final result.
