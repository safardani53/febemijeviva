TWO Strictly Come Dancing hunks looked unrecognisable in drag as they filmed a Channel 4 reality series - while two more celebs are revealed.

Celebrity Hunted is back on Channel 4 for another Stand Up to Cancer series, with a fresh batch of famous faces trying to avoid being caught by the hunters.

Last week, The Sun revealed Strictly Come Dancing pros Giovanni Pernice and Kai Widdrington were one of the celeb pairs taking part.

Now, the hunks have been seen filming the show for the first time, but fans could be forgiven for not recognising them, considering they were in full drag.

Kai, 27, and Giovanni, 32, headed to Wales over the weekend and filmed a video taunting the hunters at The Golden Cross venue.

Gio wore a bouffant brown wig, huge earrings, full make up over his beard and a glittering gown with feathered cuffs, while Kai opted for a granny chic ensemble complete with white high neck blouse, pale blue cardigan, glasses and a dusty blonde wig.

Teasing the hunters, Kai said: "Good afternoon to Ray and all the hunters, hope you're all doing very well."

Gio then gave a flamboyant "Hi!" before adding: "Well we have just finished our performance in Cardiff and yeah, we can't see any hunters around, where are you?"

Kai replied that "no one was waiting for us" and Giovanni said "well that's a shame".

He continued: "Hope you enjoy the show if you're watching the CCTV because we recorded everything for you. Enjoy!"

The pair then blew a kiss to the camera, which is sure to annoy the hunters even more.

The dancers weren't the only celeb pairing pictured at gay pride events this weekend, as Blue singer Duncan James and Christine McGuinness were also revealed to be part of the show.

The pair posed for pictures at Birmingham Pride with two drag queens while wearing rainbow flags around their necks and matching hats.

In the accompanying tweet, the Twitter user simply wrote: "Celebrity
@Hunted_HQ @BirminghamPride tonight!"

The two pairs will also be joined on the line-up of the Channel 4 show next year by Loose Women star Denise Welch and her husband Lincoln Townley.

Also appearing on the next series will be two former soap actresses.

Danielle Harold, who plays dying Lola Pearce in BBC soap EastEnders, will be taking on her first post-soap role on the programme.

She will be accompanied by former Corrie star Kimberley Hart-Simpson as they try to evade capture.

Celebrity Hunted airs on Channel 4.
