FIGHTER pilots have been quarantined to keep the Navy’s £3billion aircraft carrier on course for her first mission.

F35 Lightning jets will practise scrambling from HMS Queen Elizabeth’s decks against “enemy” fighters this week.

Captain Angus Essenhigh — Big Lizzie’s commander — said only pilots who tested negative for the virus would be allowed to land their jets on board.

Four F35s are due to touch down today and Capt Essenhigh, 46, said the airmen from RAF Marham, Norfolk, have been in strict isolation.

He added: “I feel absolutely assured they are in as clean a condition as we are.”

The jets come with 250 engineers and crew who joined the ship at Portsmouth over the weekend.

Big Lizzie has been Covid- free since two crew who tested positive left the ship last month.
