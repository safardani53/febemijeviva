Mark Percival ready to prove he is not as dozy as is made out

March 24, 2024
blog

MARK Percival is itching to make a big noise now he has his World Cup chance – after being the quiet man off the field.

The St Helens ace will line up at centre against France today after being overlooked for the games against Australia and Lebanon.

But it is off the field where Percival has made his biggest impact, mostly by not reacting to anything at all.

Team-mate Thomas Burgess responded when asked if he could be any of his colleagues for a day, who would it be and why: “Mark Percival, just to see what was going on in that head.

“He came into the hotel burnt to a crisp after being sat on the beach and probably falling asleep with no sun cream on.”

Now the sunburn on his legs has eased, he insists he is not as daft as people think he is, just relaxed.

“I am a quiet lad so they call me a bit dozy and say that always day dreaming,” he admitted.

“It is just the way I am. They probably see me as a bit thick but I am not that thick, I am just chilling, that is me.

“Nothing really winds me up, but the flies around Perth and Fremantle are winding me up the most. They keep coming at you, they don't stop keep flying at your head!”

Percival, 23, has another reason to be angry, the fact his girlfriend Carys flew back to the UK yesterday after spending time with him.

But his frustrations at not playing will be eased when he takes to the field this morning.

Percival added: “We had a few days off so I enjoyed the sights with Carys and her brother.

“I really appreciate that she took the time off from university, where she studies Spanish and French.

“But she has had to go back for work reasons.

“It is not really frustrating to not be picked. Wayne Bennett has picked a 24-man squad for a reason so I am enjoying it, training and watching the others play. We are all part of the same team.

“I’ve been learning off the best coach in the world and trying to improve and now to get the chance and try and take it. Looking forward to getting out there.

“Wayne told me at the mid-season Test against Samoa what I need to work on and I have kept that in mind and been trying to do that. I’m now looking forward to doing a job for him.”

Percival comes in along with Kevin Brown, Scott Taylor and Mike McMeeken, meaning all 24 players will have appeared in the group games.

But even though he has not been able to take to the field, it does not mean he has picked anything up or learned anything from coach Bennett or players like James Graham and Sam Burgess.

Showing he can adapt from rainy Sydney to scorching Perth has been just one of his tasks.

“It is watching the way they prepare for games, the likes of James and Sam are very switched on and focussed,” he admitted.

“They get the job done no matter who they are playing. It is their focus and that is what I have learned, there’s been a lot about myself as well.

“You come into training sessions and you cannot slacken off, you have got to be focussed in everything you're doing.  That's what I have learned off them the most.

“It has been different everywhere we have gone but it’s going to be a lot tougher with the heat but hopefully we can get the job done no matter what the weather.

“I have been spectating last couple of weeks and I’m a bit of both, a good watcher and a bad watcher.

“It would have been nice to play but I was just loving supporting the lads. It is 24-man squad for a reason and we're all part of it whether you’re playing or not.

“We have got to play our part. We have to play against the lads and give them the best opposition that we can to prepare if we’re not playing.

“We are all part of the same team. If you’re not playing you still have your bit to do, but it’s nice to be part of it.”
