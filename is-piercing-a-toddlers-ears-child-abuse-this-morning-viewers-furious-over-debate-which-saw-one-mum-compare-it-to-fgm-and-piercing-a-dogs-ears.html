VIEWERS of This Morning waded in on the debate over piercing toddlers’ ears after two mums went head to head over the issue – and one compared it to female genital mutilation.

Alley Einstein, who has a seven-year-old daughter, claimed that piercing a child’s ears was no different to piercing a dog’s ears.

And she even compared using culture as an excuse to behaving like “Adolf Hitler.”

Appearing with Raj Gill, who had her five-year-old’s ears pierced, Alley said: “I think we should set the time and age when a child should have it done and it should be at least 10 years of age.

“We’re forcing our children to be sexualised too early.

“We’re slamming needles into their ears. We don’t need to do that. Our children are beautiful enough.

“There is no religion or culture in this world that would ostracise a child because they haven’t got their ears pierced.”

Celebrities including Kim Kardashian and Giselle Bundchen have had their babies' ears pierced, and Raj defended her decision.

Raj argued: “It’s the parent’s prerogative. You say it’s causing damage but the government agencies say it is not any threat or danger to a child to have their ears pierced.”

But Alley jumped in with: “Do you support female genital mutilation?”

When Raj answered “Absolutely not”, Alley retorted:  “Then how you can allow your child to have its ears pierced when there’s no medical reason to have that done?

Raj said: “Calling it child abuse is ridiculous. Have you Googled child abuse?

Alley replied: “It’s inflicting unnecessary pain.”

Mum-of-two Raj told hosts Phillip Schofield and Holly Willoughby her daughter had felt no pain.

She said: “She wanted it done. If she felt any pain we would have stopped.

“She’d been asking for a long time to have it done and it’s part of our culture.

“It’s a huge thing and we go out and but jewellery together, we have jewellery made together and she’s seen this growing up and she’d been asking for a long time.”

Alley told her: “If I had a dog, and I brought him into this studio with both ears pierced I would be ostracised, and rightly so. So why do that to a child?”

Raj argued: “My child had her ears pierced at five and she’s had no problems. She had her aftercare kit and she’s looked after them."

Phillip reminded Alley: “You compared claiming it was “cultural thing to being like Adolf Hitler.”

Alley said: “Why would you inflict pain on your child?”

In a poll at the end of the show, 32 per cent of viewers said it was OK to pierce a child’s ears but 68 per cent said it wasn’t acceptable.

Viewers were split with one tweeting:  “No ear piercing until a child becomes a teenager!”

Another added: “My daughter got hers pierced at 5, her nose and belly button at 14... it’s hardly bloody child abuse ..it’s a piercing.

“Bloody do gooders. Raise your kids how you see fit and the rest of us can do the same.”

But many were angry at the reference to FGM in the debate.

Placid Madness wrote: “The moment the woman compared piercing ears to FGM she lost the argument end of.”

Happygnome added: “Trying to compare FGM to ear piercing you stupid woman!”
