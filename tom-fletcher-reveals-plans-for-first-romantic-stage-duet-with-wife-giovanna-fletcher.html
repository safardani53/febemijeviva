Tom Fletcher reveals plans for first theatre duet with wife Giovanna Fletcher for The Christmasaurus

March 30, 2024
blog

TOM Fletcher has revealed he will team up with wife Giovanna Fletcher in their sweet stage debut for the live version of his festive book The Christmasaurus.

The McFly singer seemed truly chuffed to reveal the news on today's Lorraine show.

He will be touring his kid's novel around the country next year and said: "I've got some special in the Christmasaurus live and the first one I can announce is my wife Giovanna.

"She will be performing with us next year.

"we met at theatre school and she grew up performing so she's going to be singing with me and Santa."

Host Lorraine Kelly cooed over the joint effort and said: "Ohh that will be lovely!

"We will need to come down and support her, we will all be there."

He teased fans with an Instagram video as he announced he would take the story on-stage.

Alongside the scenes he wrote: ""The Christmasaurus LIVE! Is coming to the Eventim Apollo London on Friday December 22nd 2017, featuring music, magic, me and some very special guests (to be announced)."

Popstar Tom also revealed an animated feature length movie musical was in the pipeline based on The Christmasaurus.

The plot is set on Christmas Eve and follows little boy William Trundle, an elf named Snozzletrump, Santa Claus), the Hunter ad, of course, a dinosaur.

He added: "In my head I imagined it could work on the stage or as a movie.

"It looks like we are getting to do everything which is amazing."

"The whole book started with the song I wrote The Christmasaurus.

"Buzz walks around the house singing all the songs - it's really cute."

Meanwhile mum-of-two Giovanna recently admitted that sons Buzz, two, and Buddy, eight months, keep them far too busy for any romantic endeavours, in addition to their hectic careers.

“I have no time for romance! What is that?” the 31-year-old asked The Mirror.

“We tried to go out for our anniversary at the weekend, we’ve been together as adults for 13 years and Buzz threw up everywhere, so date night cancelled, we had to go home."

She added: “We are so busy at the moment, it’s like through the night [with their youngest], and then get up with the kids at 7am, work, be with the kids, and then work again when they go to sleep until midnight.

“We’re on a rota system at the moment, it will calm down in the next two weeks.”
